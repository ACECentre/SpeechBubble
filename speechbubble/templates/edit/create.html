Hardware is all physical hardware that communication solution runs on. Think of a tablet computer or a talking button.
App is used to define all software. This includes framework software for the computer that allows you to create communication solutions and single pieces of software that run on a computer with multiple, built-in 'vocabularies'.
Vocabularies are defined, structured vocabulary packages that are included within resources. These may be symbol or word based and have a defined number of words that come as standard - ordered in a particular way. Please enter your vocabulary once your 'app' has been added.
"Low-tech" equipment means equipment that generally requires no battery or electronic screen. This includes paper based resources. Some may call this "No-Tech" resources as they generally have no powered-technology involved to use.
"Simple" resources are those resources that generally are not regarded as computer based, i.e. have a processor, power (from battery or mains-powered) and screen. This includes such items as single and fixed number message devices that generally have no digital screen. Some people also call this "light" or "low" "tech".
"Advanced" hardware means devices that generally are acting as a computer. They may have a screen, a battery or mains powered and allow for a large number of messages being created. Some people may also call this category "High-tech".
